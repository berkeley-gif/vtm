WIESLANDER VEGETATION TYPE MAPPING

In the 1920s and 1930s Albert Everett Wieslander and several others explored much of California's wilderness sampling vegetation, taking photographs, collecting specimens, and drawing detailed maps of what they found. These data represent a valuable resource for comparative and conservation ecology. Explore and download the Wieslander Vegetation Type Mapping project collection to get a snapshot of California's vegetation in the early 20th century.
